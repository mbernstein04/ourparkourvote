Why This Matters

Duluth politicians want to rezone and sell Lester Park to private developers. We believe Duluthians should have the final say — that's how democracy is supposed to work.

Who We Are

Our Park Our Vote is a not-for-profit group of Duluth residents united by one simple belief: public land decisions belong to the public. Learn more →

What We're Doing

Our Park Our Vote is collecting citizen petition signatures to force a ballot initiative. If we gather enough signatures, the city must let voters decide Lester's future.

Why It Matters

Once public land is sold, it's gone forever. Developers — not Duluthians — will get to decide its future. We need to use our voice to tell city hall this isn't what we want.

The Backstory

How Did We
Get Here?

The Mayor wants to bring golf back to Lester Park. The problem? The city can't afford it. But a private developer from Florida can — and the deal isn't what it sounds like.

The Developer's Vision

A Florida-based developer envisions a “Vail-Breckenridge” style chalet hotel on public parkland. Other courses he's developed charge up to $415 per round. This isn't community golf — it's a luxury resort built on land that belongs to Duluthians.

Why City Hall Wants the Deal

Enger Park Golf Course needs a new clubhouse, and the money from selling Lester is earmarked to build it. In other words, one public park is being sacrificed to fund improvements at another — without voters ever being asked.
