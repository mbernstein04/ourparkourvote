**Subject: Lester Park update — citizen initiative petition + Get 5 for 5**

Hi Ikes,

It was great to get face time with some of you last night. I wanted to follow up with an update on where things stand with the Lester Park effort.

I've tacked. This is our land, and the city is planning to sell it without asking us. The petition would require voter approval for any Lester rezoning from P1 to anything else. I've updated the initiative website to reflect the shift.

I did it for a few strategic reasons:

1. **It forces the issue.** A citizen initiative petition isn't an ask of the mayor. It's a legal mechanism — the right to vote on the future of our own public land.

2. **It gives us more time.** We have until the City attempts the rezoning — November? December? — to collect the signatures we need. That's a much longer runway than a traditional petition.

3. **Two bites at the apple.** We need roughly 3,664 hard-copy voter signatures. If we don't hit that number before the rezoning vote, the list we've built gives us a strong base to launch a recall effort within 30 days of any council rezoning. Either way, the signatures count.

**To make this work, I'm launching a volunteer program called "Get 5 for 5."**

The easiest volunteer gig you'll ever have — with an enticing reward for participating.

No scripts. No door-knocking. No awkward conversations with strangers who aren't home anyway.

Here's the whole thing: **get 5 people to sign a hard copy of the petition every week.** Friends. Neighbors. People you already talk to. That's it. Download it, print it, get it signed in person.

Every Sunday at **5:55 PM**, we hop on a **5-minute** conference call. We go around: did you get your 5? If not, you give your excuse — "I was too busy" or "I wasn't in the mood" or "I was sick" are all legitimate excuses. We might even ask if we could bring you some soup. And not to get sappy, but we're envisioning whole families on the call. It's an easy way to teach your kids about democratic participation and volunteering. Then we hang up. Five minutes. Done.

Oh, and for every Sunday call you show up to, you'll receive a **Baker's 5-Pack** of native plants — that I'm growing right here in my basement. Baker's 5-Packs will be distributed at a house party on April 5th. The first fifty people to get in on this thing will get priority for the Baker's 5-Packs. The others will get as many as there are left.

The goal: **50 people** on the Get 5 call by April 5th. That's 50 people each getting 5 signatures a week.

The real goal is simple: save 230 acres of parkland. Wouldn't it be something — to be a small but very tangible and direct part of giving 230 acres back to nature? All you have to do is Get 5.

You can sign up at [ourparkourvote.org](https://ourparkourvote.org), or just email me at info@ourparkourvote.org.

Anyway, I look forward to talking with at least some of you about the initiative later this week.

Best,
Michael
